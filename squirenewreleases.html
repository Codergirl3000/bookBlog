Squire by Nadia Shammas and Sara Alfageeh

Bella - Mon Jul 3, 2023

Introduction

Squire is a book about a girl who is from a “lower class” because of her ethnicity. This girl decides the only way to escape this social status is to join the army, and when she joins the army she must hide herself to fit in. But when it comes to it, she must decide whether or not to stay true to her culture, or her nation.

What's Different About This Book?

I really liked this book because it was in Graphic Novel format, which not many books are written in this form that go into so much detail. Squire is a good book because the book isn’t just a surface story, it tells the story of a girl who is facing social pressures because of something out of her control. Much like many people in the world today, and the author does a great job of covering that topic while not having to directly address that topic.

What Did I Like About This Book?

I loved this book because it was a great quick read for me, and I just needed to read something while waiting for my book at the library, and this was an unexpectedly great book. A small quaint book that was better than expected, a pleasant surprise. This went into more detail than many other graphic novels I’ve read. The graphic also went well with the story and the vibe it gave me fit really well with the storyline.

Overall Rating

I would rate this book a strict 7.95/10 because I feel it could have gone deeper into the story, but also was a great introduction into this world. This book was a great quick read, I read it in one sitting, about 2 and a half hours. A great book to read when you're bored and want to read a quick book.
